About Me
Also known by my friends as Zam, my stage name ZamKim, or my legal name and to Koreans as Eun Soo Kim (김은수).
I grew up in El Salvador between the ages of two and eighteen attending a British school. I am Korean by birth, raised by Korean parents. Due to all of this, my head naturally operates in a messy bleed of English, Spanish, and Korean—and now, more than ever, I'm unable to honestly say what my strongest language is. Having spent my formative years inside such varied cultures, physical geography has never dictated who or what I am; my internal world does.
When I want to talk to the world, I make music. I enjoy building soundscapes where other people can live, mostly with the aim of offering someone the same honest lifeline that music once gave me. But when I need to talk to myself, I write poetry. It’s quieter, messier, and strictly a coping mechanism—a place to take an abstract thought and try to paint it into an object just to see if it holds its shape. This space is strictly for the latter.
The daily project is something I started to develop my own literary language and world—hence why a lot of it will feel rough. It runs on prompts generated by a system I set up, using constraints that evolve over time. Other than "the project", I'll occasionally write freely about whatever I'm feeling at the moment, and probably publish the ones I feel are ready to say hi to the world.
Read whatever you want. Take what you need.
