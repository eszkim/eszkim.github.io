Some moments are measured by the traces they leave behind—or the ones they don't.
I feel the bead of sweat Sliding down my temple, And then tracing the path Along the ridges of my spine. Pulses beneath my skin, Mapping hidden streams. Blood rushes through the veins, Turning warmth to fever. The rough texture of the fabric On the collar of my shirt Scratches against my neck, Presses against my jugular. Humidity fills the air— Perspiration, heavy breaths— Until the atmosphere Makes skin cling to skin. A whisper of rose perfume Feeds anticipation. I breathe it in slowly; Hair stands on end. Fingers hover a heartbeat, Unable to close the distance. As they drift ever closer Static bridges the empty space. A single breath hangs in the air, A second turns into three years. A single blink of an eye Renders me completely blind. The taste of steel on tongue, Lips bit tightly shut, Lest silence be disturbed Or night retain the evidence. Then silence so apparent, Filled only by panting. The windows are fogged up, Fingerprints remain.
